# Dimensional System — Report Content & Structure Brief

This document specifies *what* the report contains, in what sequence, with the actual text to render. It contains no design or visual direction — that will be appended separately.

All copy below is final, production-quality, and rendered as written.

---

## What this product is

A precision psychological diagnostic. It produces a personalised reading of an individual's psychological architecture across three foundational domains — **Safety, Play, Challenge** — and their six dimensions. It is a diagnostic instrument, not a personality test, not a wellness assessment, not a coaching tool.

The reading is individual and phenomenological: no population norms, no ranking, no comparison between people. It is a snapshot of one person's current configuration, intended to produce self-recognition.

## The framework being measured

Three foundational domains, each scored 0–100 and given a band. Order is always Safety → Play → Challenge.

Each domain has two dimensions (also scored 0–100):
- Safety → Self, Others
- Play → Senses, Perception
- Challenge → Past, Future

Each domain and dimension is read in two states: **Felt** (internal) and **Expressed** (outward). The distance between them is the **alignment** reading; a significant gap is a key finding.

Bands: Very Low · Low · Almost Balanced · Balanced · Slightly Excessive · Excess.

The relationship between the three domain scores produces a named **shape** (e.g. Sharp Peak).

## Sequence

Seven sections plus an outro, in fixed order:

1. Orientation
2. Your Shape — the reveal of the pattern, the configuration named, and a short reading of what that shape tends to produce
3. Safety
4. Play
5. Challenge
6. How Your Foundations Work Together — the synthesis: the three domains as one interacting system, setting up the lever
7. Your Foundation Direction
8. When You're Ready (outro)

Each domain section (3, 4, 5) follows the same internal structure:
- Score and band
- What [domain] is — a brief, factual definition of the domain and its two dimensions
- What your level means — what low, balanced, and excess look like in plain behavioural terms, then where this reader falls
- Role in your shape
- Felt — what the domain feels like inside
- Expressed — what others see
- Alignment finding
- Dimension one (score, band, reading)
- Dimension two (score, band, reading)

Two elements receive a flagged treatment for this reader: the Alignment Finding in Section 3 (Safety), and the Future dimension in Section 5 (Challenge).

Purpose: orient and disarm, plant the core concept (psychological foundations), and set the posture for reading — without instructing the reader how to feel and without showing any scores yet. Scores and shape belong to Section 2 (Overview). The interventions-vs-foundations argument is deliberately held back to Section 7.

Structure on the page: a short disarming intro → three concept points (each an icon + header + one line) → the posture beat → handoff into the Overview. Not a block of text. The three concept points carry the weight and must be scannable.

**Intro — what this is** (one or two lines, human voice)

This is a reading of what sits beneath how you think, feel, and behave. A diagnostic, not a personality test — and not a verdict on who you are.

**Three concept points** (icon + header + one line each)

1. *Beneath everything* — Your daily thoughts, feelings, and behaviour rest on three psychological foundations: Safety, Play, and Challenge.
2. *They work together* — How these three interact shapes how you experience your life. When one runs low, the others are left to carry more.
3. *They can be built* — Foundations are not fixed. With diagnostic clarity, they can be built faster than people expect — and this reading shows you precisely where yours stand.

**Posture beat** (states what the reading is; does not instruct how to feel)

This captures the season you are in, not a fixed essence or a passing mood. It is not about becoming someone new. It is about seeing what already is.

**Handoff** (hands the reader agency, then the entry point)

Take what recognises you. Leave what doesn't. — [button: See your shape →]

## Section 2 — Your Shape

The reveal. The symbol lands first (the pattern, visually), then it is named as a configuration, then a short reading of what that shape tends to produce. The attention layer follows. Within the section, let the shape land on its own before the naming and meaning appear — the reveal should not be crowded.

The symbol has two views: domains and dimensions.

**Symbol tooltips — domains view**
- Safety: "Safety — Very Low. Your foundation here is running thin."
- Play: "Play — Low. The part of you that replenishes is operating on reserve."
- Challenge: "Challenge — High. Direction and pursuit are doing real work in your life."

**Symbol tooltips — dimensions view**
- Self: "Self — Low. Your friendship with yourself is conditional right now."
- Others: "Others — Very Low. Leaning on people is harder than it looks from the outside."
- Senses: "Senses — Low. Your body is somewhere just outside the room."
- Perception: "Perception — Almost Balanced. Your mental flexibility is present, if somewhat narrowed."
- Past: "Past — Almost Balanced. Your history is mostly integrated, with some chapters still loose."
- Future: "Future — High. You are oriented forward with real clarity."

**Your configuration — A Sharp Peak** (the shape named)

A Sharp Peak appears when one domain rises clearly above the other two. The peak is not accidental. It is doing structural work the depleted domains cannot, and the wider the gap between the apex and the base, the more compensatory the shape becomes. A Sharp Peak is rarely a sign that one domain has grown too tall. It is a sign that the others have not been able to hold their share.

**What this shape tends to produce** (the brief reading — generic to the pattern, not yet the personal synthesis)

A person with a Sharp Peak feels most like themselves at the apex. The peak generates the experience the other domains cannot — significance, momentum, relief, recognition, the sense that life is moving. This works, until it doesn't. Because the peak carries the weight of three domains, it is asked to provide what it was not designed to provide alone. The body is asked to feel safe through achievement. The nervous system is asked to rest through productivity. Connection is asked to come through being needed.

Over time, this shape produces a particular kind of fatigue. Not the fatigue of working too hard, which a Sharp Peak person often welcomes. The fatigue of asking a single part of yourself to do work it cannot finish. From the outside, a Sharp Peak often looks like high performance. From the inside, it often feels like running with one functioning engine and learning not to ask why.

**Attention layer — the four flags** (each clickable; destination in brackets)

1. Your Safety reads as Very Low, and the gap between how it feels inside and how you express it is significant — what looks composed from the outside is not what you are carrying within. [→ Alignment Finding, Section 3]
2. Your Challenge sits high and aligned. It is a clear resource, and the domain doing most of the work in your overall shape. [→ Section 5]
3. Your Future dimension reads notably high against the rest of your reading — you are oriented strongly forward, even as your foundations sit thin. [→ Future dimension, Section 5]
4. Your shape is a Sharp Peak — one domain holding the structural weight while the other two run quietly below. [→ what this shape tends to produce, above]

## Section 3 — Safety

**Score and band**

Safety — 27% — Very Low.

**What Safety is**

Safety is the foundation of inner peace — what lets you feel grounded from within and meet life without defence. It has two dimensions: your relationship with yourself (Self) and your relationship with others (Others).

**What your level means**

Low safety is not always obvious. It often shows itself through over-controlling, driving too hard, or holding a composure that takes effort to maintain. Underneath, there is usually a background sense that something could go wrong, a habit of watching how others react, and rest that does not fully restore.

When safety is balanced, you feel at ease without needing to manage it — steady in yourself when things are uncertain, calm in your body without conditions, and able to be close to people without monitoring or performing.

In excess, safety tips the other way: so much weight on staying settled that you can feel numb, and stepping into anything new or stimulating starts to feel like a risk you would rather not take.

Your reading sits at the very low end of this scale. What follows is how that reads for you — first inside, then outwardly, then across Self and Others.

**Role in your shape**

Safety is the depleted leg of your Sharp Peak — the foundation Challenge has been quietly compensating for.

**Felt Safety — what this domain feels like inside**

At this level, your nervous system is rarely off duty. Even in moments that should feel safe, there is a quiet vigilance running underneath — the sense that something needs watching, that ease might not last, that you have to stay ready. Rest does not fully restore you. Your inner voice is often the loudest voice in the room, and it is rarely on your side. You may not call any of this anxiety, because it does not look dramatic from the outside. It is just the texture you have learned to live in.

**Expressed Safety — what others see**

Outwardly, you express Safety at 35%. You hold yourself together. You arrive on time, you meet your obligations, you keep your composure under pressure. Most of the people around you would describe you as steady, even reliable. What they do not see is what the steadiness costs, or that the composure is held by effort rather than ease. Your expression of Safety is slightly steadier than what you actually feel — and that gap is doing its own kind of work.

**Alignment finding** (flagged for this reader)

Your felt Safety reads 22%; your expressed Safety reads 35%. This is a significant upward divergence — you appear more settled than you actually feel, and the gap is wide enough that it is no longer a small piece of social management. It is structural. You have learned to look safer than you are, and the effort of that maintenance is a hidden cost most readings would not surface.

The cost of carrying this gap is not only the energy of the performance. It is the slow erosion of the link between your inner experience and the people around you. The colleagues, friends, and partners who think you are fine are not wrong — you are showing them fine. But fine is not what is happening. The longer the gap persists, the harder it becomes to be received as you actually are. Over time, the people closest to you may know you less than they think.

**Self — your dimension reading**

Self — 38% — Low.

Your relationship with yourself is conditional. You approve of yourself when you have produced something, when you have held the line under pressure, when you have been the version of you that meets your own standards. When you have not, the approval is withdrawn quickly. Your self-talk tends to be sharper than the way you would speak to anyone else in your life, and the sharpness is most reliable when you are tired or when something has not gone as you expected.

From the inside, this often looks like self-discipline. From the architecture, it reads as a foundation that requires payment for entry. You can rest with yourself, but only after the work is finished. You can be on your own side, but only when you have earned it. This is the kind of inner relationship that holds up well in achievement and falters in stillness.

**Others — your dimension reading**

Others — 24% — Very Low.

Leaning on people is something you do rarely, and rarely without internal accounting. Support, when it comes, is harder to receive than to offer. You may have people who would be there for you, and you may not have asked them to be — not because you do not trust them, but because asking carries a quiet weight you would rather not transfer. Connection with you often runs through what you provide rather than what you need.

This is not isolation. From the outside, you are likely well-regarded, often consulted, sometimes leaned on by others. The architecture beneath that is a one-way relationship to interdependence. You give support more easily than you take it, and the ratio is wide enough that the people who would gladly hold some of your weight are mostly unaware you are carrying it.

## Section 4 — Play

**Score and band**

Play — 41% — Low.

**What Play is**

Play is vibrant, felt aliveness — what brings lightness, joy, and richness to your experience of life. It has two dimensions: your relationship with your body (Senses) and your relationship with your mind (Perception).

**What your level means**

Low play is not always obvious. It often shows itself through life feeling flat or mechanical, a quiet guilt around doing things purely for enjoyment, and a disconnection from the body's signals and pleasures. Spontaneity, surprise, and lightness meet resistance, and joy can feel distant or unearned.

When play is balanced, there is a sense of lightness woven through ordinary days — enjoyment in sensory and creative experience, regular moments of laughter or flow, and energy that feels alive without needing a reason.

In excess, play loses its anchor: immersion tips into over-indulgence, the search for stimulation outpaces actual pleasure, and you can lose yourself in what feels good at the cost of your other needs.

Your reading sits at the low end of this scale. What follows is how that reads for you — first inside, then outwardly, then across Senses and Perception.

**Role in your shape**

Play sits low alongside Safety — the part of you that would normally replenish is not generating much right now.

**Felt Play — what this domain feels like inside**

Your inner life has narrowed. The textures that would normally arrive without effort — the small flickers of curiosity, the body responding to a piece of music, the moment of being absorbed in something for its own sake — show up less than they used to, and you may not have noticed. You can describe your days, but fewer of them have flavour. You can name what you did, but less of it left an impression on you.

**Expressed Play — what others see**

Outwardly, you express Play at 47%. You laugh in the right places, you enjoy meals with people, you do the things that look like rest and pleasure. From a distance, your life appears to contain enjoyment. It does — just less than it appears to, and less than is available. The gap between what you feel and what you show is not large, but it is present.

**Alignment finding**

Your felt Play reads 38%; your expressed Play reads 47%. This is a mild upward divergence — you express slightly more ease and pleasure than you currently feel. The gap is not architecturally significant on its own, but read alongside your Safety alignment, it suggests a consistent pattern. You are working harder than is visible to look more settled than you are.

**Senses — your dimension reading**

Senses — 39% — Low.

Your body is somewhere just outside the room. You inhabit it functionally — you move through your day, you eat, you sleep — but the small portals through which Play normally enters have narrowed. Warmth, taste, texture, breath, the weight of someone you love resting on you — these arrive as data more than as experience. You can register that something is pleasant without being filled by it. The body that should be a daily source of replenishment is currently a vehicle.

This is not numbness. It is a body running a low-power mode it adopted to manage something. The disengagement is not a flaw in you. It is the result of asking your senses to do less so something else could carry more. Over time, this is the dimension most likely to recover quickly when given small, deliberate attention — but in this reading, it is not currently doing the work it is capable of.

**Perception — your dimension reading**

Perception — 43% — Almost Balanced.

Your mental flexibility is present, but operating with less curiosity than is natural to you. You can hold complexity, you can shift perspective when asked, and you are not rigid in your thinking — but you are not currently generating new angles either. Interpretation has become more dutiful than playful. Your inner perceptual world is in maintenance mode, doing what is needed without the looseness that produces fresh insight.

This is a quieter dimension in your reading, neither the strongest nor the most depleted. What it tells you is that the mental capacity is still there. It is the body's invitation to engage that has narrowed — and Perception, with less to receive from the senses, is generating less of what it would otherwise generate. The two halves of Play work together. When one runs low, the other compensates for a time, and then both quietly contract.

## Section 5 — Challenge

**Score and band**

Challenge — 78% — High.

**What Challenge is**

Challenge is meaningful forward motion — what gives you direction, purpose, and the sense that your actions matter. It has two dimensions: your relationship with your past (Past) and your relationship with your future (Future).

**What your level means**

Low challenge is not always obvious. It often shows itself through a sense of stagnation, avoiding decisions that carry real weight, and routines that stay busy while crowding out meaning. There is frequently a tension between what you want and what you actually move on, and a quiet loss of momentum or direction.

When challenge is balanced, there is clear direction that is personally meaningful — movement toward goals with self-respect, motivation that feels rooted rather than forced, and pride in how the challenges are chosen and carried.

In excess, challenge becomes relentless: struggle gets mistaken for meaning, busyness masquerades as growth, and you can chase goals you do not genuinely care about or find it hard to stop.

Your reading sits at the high end of this scale. What follows is how that reads for you — first inside, then outwardly, then across Past and Future.

**Role in your shape**

Challenge is the apex of your Sharp Peak — the domain holding most of your psychological weight, generating significance the other two cannot currently provide.

**Felt Challenge — what this domain feels like inside**

Direction is one of the most settled things in your life. You know what you are doing, you know why you are doing it, and the connection between your effort and your meaning is strong. Pursuit feels good in your body. Forward motion regulates you in a way that few other things do. When you set a goal that matters, your system organises around it quickly, and that organisation is itself a source of relief.

**Expressed Challenge — what others see**

Outwardly, you express Challenge at 77% — almost exactly what you feel. Others would describe you as driven, focused, often ambitious. They would say you are someone who follows through. The match between the inner and outer experience here is real. This is the part of your architecture where what you feel and what you show are the same thing.

**Alignment finding**

Your felt Challenge reads 75%; your expressed Challenge reads 77%. This is an aligned reading. The drive you are showing the world is the drive you actually feel. There is no performance here. In a reading otherwise marked by alignment gaps, this domain is doing something important — it is the part of you that is fully whole, and the part you can most reliably trust.

**Past — your dimension reading**

Past — 51% — Almost Balanced.

Your history is mostly integrated. You have a working relationship with where you have been, and you are not haunted by your story — but there are still some chapters that have not fully been authored. You can describe what happened, but a few pieces of it remain on the shelf rather than woven into your identity. The narrative is largely yours, with some loose ends.

This is not a depleted dimension. It is a foundation that is functional and not yet finished. What is interesting in your reading is that your Future runs significantly higher than your Past — meaning you are oriented forward more strongly than you are anchored backward. For most people this would be precarious. In your reading, it suggests that the past is doing enough to let the future do its job, even if some of the integration work remains.

**Future — your dimension reading** (flagged for this reader)

Future — 82% — High.

You are oriented forward with real clarity. Your sense of direction is one of the most resourced parts of your entire architecture — you know where you are going, you have planned the next few steps, and the future you are working toward is one you actually want. Direction is not a struggle for you. Choosing what to pursue is not where your energy gets stuck.

This is also, read against your other scores, the most architecturally interesting part of your reading. Your Future dimension is doing significant work. It is pulling you forward into a life that has not yet arrived, while your Safety foundations sit thin underneath. Strong forward orientation can be a real resource, and in your reading it is. It can also become its own form of compensation — a place to live mentally that asks less of the foundations than the present moment does. Worth holding both of those readings together as you sit with this.

## Section 6 — How Your Foundations Work Together

The synthesis. The reader has now met all three domains in depth. This section reads them as one interacting system — what the three are doing to and for each other — and sets up the lever that follows in Section 7. This is the integrated view the earlier sections could not yet support.

**Your three, as one system**

Your peak is Challenge, and it sits at 78% with the felt and expressed states almost identical. This is real. You are oriented forward, you find significance in pursuit, and what you do in the world is genuinely connected to what matters to you. Your Future dimension at 82% reinforces this — you have a clear sense of direction, and that direction is yours.

Beneath the peak, your Safety reads at 27% with a wide alignment gap. You feel less safe than you appear, and the difference is significant enough that you carry a quiet load others do not see. Your Play sits at 41%, neither resourced nor catastrophic, but unable to replenish what Safety drains. The result is a system where Challenge generates momentum, Safety quietly depletes, and Play does not have enough room to refill what is leaving.

What this produces in your life is the experience of running well and not knowing why rest does not feel like rest. The peak is doing more than its share. Your reading does not tell you to lower the peak. It tells you the peak is currently the only thing holding the whole system up — and that is why the direction your reading points is not toward the peak, but toward the foundation beneath it.

## Section 7 — Your Foundation Direction

**Domain**

Safety.

**Opening**

Your reading points to Safety as the domain where strengthening would produce the most architectural change for you. Safety is the depleted leg of your Sharp Peak — the foundation Challenge has been quietly compensating for. Building here would shift not just Safety itself, but how the rest of your shape can finally rest.

**Why this works — foundations, not interventions** (the argument, earned here because the reader has now seen their own reading)

If you have ever set out to change something — your wellbeing, your leadership, your relationships — you have been handed interventions. A hundred different techniques, habits, and tactics, each promising to fix the thing on the surface. Most do not hold, because they work above the foundation rather than on it.

This is not an intervention. It is the foundation the interventions were trying to reach. And because this reading is precise — it shows you exactly which foundation, and exactly where it sits — building here has a higher chance of working, and of working faster, than another technique laid over the top. That is the difference between managing the surface and strengthening the base.

**Slider state 1 — Small change**

With small, consistent practice, the texture of your Safety begins to change. The inner voice softens slightly. Rest produces a little more recovery than it currently does. You notice you can be supported without immediately rebalancing the relationship by giving back. Your Challenge can ease its compensation by a small margin — you push from a slightly more grounded place rather than from the same urgent edge. Play begins to find small pockets of room. The overall shape stays recognisably yours, but the alignment gap in Safety narrows from significant to mild. You still appear steady to others. You start to actually feel more of what they see.

**Slider state 2 — Medium change**

With sustained intentional work over several months, Safety becomes a real foundation rather than the depleted leg. You rest differently. You hold yourself with less conditionality. You let people lean on you and let yourself lean back, and the asymmetry that has marked your relationships starts to even out. Your Challenge stops carrying the weight of three domains. Your Future remains a resource, but it is no longer doing emotional work the present should be doing. The architecture becomes more harmonious — the outer circle of your shape rounds out — and you start to notice that you do not need to push as hard to feel like yourself. The relief comes not from achieving more, but from needing less compensation to feel whole.

**Slider state 3 — Large change**

When Safety becomes a true resource, the whole architecture changes shape. Challenge becomes pursuit instead of compensation — the work you do is fed by foundations rather than substituting for them. Play returns, and with it the part of you that experiences pleasure as a daily presence rather than a scheduled relief. Safety holds you in a way it has not before. You sleep differently. You receive differently. The gap between what you feel and what you show closes, and the people closest to you begin to know you as you actually are, not as the steady version you have been holding.

Your shape, at this point, is no longer a Sharp Peak. The three domains move closer to each other, the peak softens because it no longer needs to be the only structural element holding everything up, and the outline of your architecture becomes nearly circular. This is foresight, not promise. You could choose this, and here is what it would become.

**Traps — patterns that look like building Safety but do not** (each renders as a distinct card)

*Overworking Challenge as a substitute*
Pushing harder at meaningful goals can feel like progress, and for a person with your reading, it almost always feels like the right move. Achievement does not build Safety. It hides the absence of Safety by giving the system enough momentum to keep going without it. The harder you push at Challenge, the longer Safety can remain depleted without you noticing. This is the most reliable trap for a Sharp Peak with Challenge at the apex.

*Performing wellness routines*
Meditation, breathwork, journaling, the morning practice — these can build Safety when they are a real relationship with yourself. They can also become another form of expression that widens the alignment gap rather than closing it. If the practice is performed, tracked, optimised, or quietly judged, it is doing the work of Expressed Safety. It is not reaching Felt Safety. The test is not whether you do the practice. It is whether you can do the practice imperfectly and still receive yourself afterwards.

*Seeking belonging through being needed*
Your Others dimension is the lowest of your six, and the most likely pathway you have built around it is to be the person others come to. Being needed produces a kind of connection that can feel like Others is building, and for the person who relies on it, it does provide something. It does not build Others. Being needed is a role. The dimension grows through being held without a role, and that is the harder thing to receive.

*Building Future faster*
Your Future dimension is high. A clearer plan, a sharper vision, a more articulate sense of what is next can feel like solid ground. It is not the same as Safety. Direction without foundations becomes another performance of competence — a way to know who you are by where you are going. Worth noticing that for you, the next strategic move is a familiar instrument. Reaching for it when this reading lands is its own kind of pattern.

## When You're Ready (outro)

This reading is the foundation. It shows what is — your psychological architecture as it sits in this season. If you want to see how this architecture shows up in specific contexts — leadership, parenting, relationships, wellbeing — secondary reports apply this same reading through those lenses. If you want to actually build, the development product teaches the practice of strengthening psychological foundations. And if you are holding this reading on your own, it is specific enough to bring to a therapist or coach who knows you.

This reading captures a season. Retesting after intentional work, or after a meaningful life shift, is its own practice.
